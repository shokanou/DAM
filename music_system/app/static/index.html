Introduction To Britpop

Britpop, which originated in the United Kingdom, is a subgenre of alternative rock music. The rise of Britpop from the British independent music scene dated back to early 1990s, and soon it became an unstoppable Britpop Movement. The bands which characterized the movement were deeply influenced by the British guitar-pop music of the 1960s and 1970s.

Introduction To J-Rock

In the 1960s, many Japanese rock bands were influenced by Western rock musicians such as The Beatles, Bob Dylan, and the Rolling Stones, along with other Appalachian folk music, psychedelic rock, mod and similar genres: a phenomenon that was called Group Sounds (G.S.).
